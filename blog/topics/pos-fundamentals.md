# POS Fundamentals

---

### POS-001 What a POS Actually Does
- **Difficulty:** beginner
- **Durations:** 1 min, 3 min, 5 min
- **Core thesis:** A POS is not just a cash register — it's the central nervous system of a restaurant, touching every part of operations from orders to inventory to reporting.
- **Key talking points:**
  1. The evolution from mechanical cash registers to cloud-based systems
  2. What a modern POS handles: order entry, payment processing, menu management, reporting, integrations
  3. Why the POS is the single most-used piece of technology in a restaurant
  4. The difference between a POS and a payment terminal (they're not the same thing)
- **Real-world hooks:** Pathfinder as DoorDash's POS play — why a delivery company built a POS. Restaurant owners who still use pen-and-paper and what they're missing.
- **Landmine to avoid:** Don't get lost in technical architecture. Keep it about the restaurant operator's experience.

### POS-002 Cloud POS vs Legacy POS
- **Difficulty:** intermediate
- **Durations:** 1 min, 3 min, 5 min
- **Core thesis:** Cloud POS systems fundamentally changed the restaurant industry by making enterprise-grade tools accessible to independent operators, but the migration is still messy and incomplete.
- **Key talking points:**
  1. Legacy POS (on-premise servers, local databases, manual updates) vs cloud (internet-dependent, real-time sync, automatic updates)
  2. The cost model shift: large upfront license fees vs SaaS subscriptions
  3. Why some restaurants resist switching — data migration, staff retraining, reliability fears
  4. The hybrid reality: most "cloud" systems still need local hardware for offline mode
- **Real-world hooks:** Toast, Square, Clover disrupting the legacy players (Aloha, Micros). Pathfinder's cloud-first architecture. Restaurants that lost data when their legacy server died.
- **Landmine to avoid:** Don't trash legacy systems — many still work fine. The point is about trade-offs, not superiority.

### POS-003 Why POS Is the Operating System of a Restaurant
- **Difficulty:** advanced
- **Durations:** 3 min, 5 min
- **Core thesis:** The POS has become the platform layer that every other restaurant technology plugs into, making it the most strategically important piece of the restaurant tech stack.
- **Key talking points:**
  1. The POS as integration hub: online ordering, delivery, loyalty, kitchen displays, inventory, payroll, accounting
  2. Platform economics: whoever controls the POS controls the data and the ecosystem
  3. Why DoorDash, Uber, and other marketplaces all want POS integrations (or to BE the POS)
  4. The tension between best-of-breed (pick the best tool for each job) and all-in-one (one vendor does everything)
  5. What happens when the POS goes down — the entire restaurant stops
- **Real-world hooks:** DoorDash building Pathfinder to own the in-store layer. Toast's ecosystem strategy. The Square/Weebly/Cash App platform play. Restaurant owners choosing their POS like choosing an operating system.
- **Landmine to avoid:** Don't overstate the "platform" analogy — restaurants aren't tech companies, and operators care about simplicity, not ecosystems.

### POS-004 The Card Reader Problem
- **Difficulty:** beginner
- **Durations:** 1 min, 3 min
- **Core thesis:** Card readers are deceptively simple devices that cause an outsized share of restaurant tech support issues, and the reliability gap between good and bad hardware directly impacts revenue.
- **Key talking points:**
  1. What a card reader actually does: NFC/chip/swipe → encryption → processor → authorization
  2. Common failure modes: disconnection, chip read errors, battery death, firmware bugs
  3. Why restaurants can't just "use another one" — readers are paired to specific POS terminals
  4. The support burden: card reader issues are the #1 inbound category for many POS companies
- **Real-world hooks:** Pathfinder's transition from WisePOS (legacy) to M2 Puck (current). The USB port color saga (blue vs orange on Elo devices). How a single dead reader at a busy lunch rush costs real money.
- **Landmine to avoid:** Don't get into Stripe internals or payment processing architecture — keep it practical and operator-focused.
