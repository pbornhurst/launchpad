# Speaking Practice Review Rubric

Scoring: 1-5 per dimension. Total possible: 25.

---

## 1. Content & Structure (1-5)

- **5:** Clear thesis stated upfront. Logical flow with smooth transitions. Supporting evidence or examples for every point. Strong conclusion that ties back to thesis.
- **4:** Thesis present. Good structure with minor gaps. Most points supported.
- **3:** Structure exists but meanders. Some points lack support. Conclusion is weak or missing.
- **2:** No clear thesis. Points feel random. Hard to follow the thread.
- **1:** Incoherent or factually wrong. No discernible structure.

## 2. Delivery & Pace (1-5)

- **5:** Natural pace with intentional pauses. Varies speed for emphasis. No rushed sections or dead air. Energy matches content.
- **4:** Mostly even pace. Minor rushing or dragging. Pauses present but not always intentional.
- **3:** Noticeably too fast or too slow in places. Some awkward pauses.
- **2:** Pace undermines comprehension. Monotone or frantic throughout.
- **1:** Unlistenable pace issues.

## 3. Clarity & Filler Words (1-5)

- **5:** Crisp articulation. Zero or near-zero filler words (um, uh, like, you know, so, right). Every sentence lands cleanly.
- **4:** Occasional fillers (under 5). Clear overall. Minor verbal tics.
- **3:** Moderate fillers (5-10). Some sentences muddled. Still comprehensible.
- **2:** Heavy fillers (10+). Sentences frequently restarted or abandoned.
- **1:** Fillers dominate. Hard to extract meaning.

## 4. Persuasiveness & Confidence (1-5)

- **5:** Would land in a boardroom. Speaker sounds like an authority. Claims are backed by evidence. Tone conveys conviction without arrogance.
- **4:** Convincing to a general audience. Occasional hedging but mostly confident.
- **3:** Makes reasonable points but delivery undercuts authority. Some hedging language ("I think maybe...").
- **2:** Sounds unsure of own material. Excessive qualifiers. Would not persuade a skeptic.
- **1:** No persuasive force. Reads as unprepared.

## 5. Engagement & Relatability (1-5)

- **5:** Uses stories, analogies, or concrete examples that make abstract concepts vivid. Audience would lean in. Has a "hook" moment.
- **4:** Some good examples or analogies. Generally engaging.
- **3:** Mostly abstract. A few concrete moments but not enough to sustain interest.
- **2:** Dry and academic. No stories or relatable moments.
- **1:** Would lose the audience immediately.

---

## Score Bands

| Range | Band | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| 21-25 | Excellent | Ready for a real audience. |
| 16-20 | Strong | Specific areas to sharpen. |
| 11-15 | Developing | Good raw material, needs structural work. |
| 6-10 | Early | Focus on one dimension at a time. |
| 1-5 | Starting | Practice shorter durations first. |

---

## Required Feedback Format

Every review must include:
1. Scores for all 5 dimensions with one-line justification each
2. Total score and band
3. Top strength (what worked best)
4. Top weakness (single most impactful thing to improve)
5. Strongest moment (verbatim quote + why it worked)
6. Weakest moment (verbatim quote or description + what to do instead)
7. Three specific, actionable next-steps
8. Comparison to previous session (if available from tracking sheet)
