Executive Summary

On fully discounted (100%-off) in-store orders, the Pathfinder POS was incorrectly labeling the payment method as cash rather than external. This inflated the cash balance expected at end-of-shift, and staff covered the shortfall from their tips to reconcile the drawer.

Engineering has identified the root-cause code path, merged a fix, and committed to rolling it to Eggsactly on or before April 30, 2026. In the interim, Phil configured a new EXTERNAL Revenue Center on the POS; running all comps through that path immediately bypasses the bug.

A full audit of the Aug 18, 2025 – Apr 16, 2026 transaction export identified 21 mislabeled orders totaling $219.50, all within a narrow 8-week window. This is the proposed reimbursement amount.

What Actually Happened

  • The bug: On fully discounted in-store orders, the POS auto-assigned payment method = cash with no option for the staff to choose another form.
  • The impact: Each mislabeled order added its subtotal to the expected cash drawer balance at close, even though no actual cash was collected. When the drawer came up short, staff dropped additional tips to close the gap.
  • Interim workaround (in place now): Phil set up a new EXTERNAL payment Revenue Center on the POS. All future comps run through that RC will bypass the bug entirely.
  • Permanent fix: Faiza (DoorDash Pathfinder Engineering) has merged a code change. It will ship to Eggsactly's POS by April 30, 2026, eliminating the need for the RC workaround thereafter.
