## Executive Summary

Cherry Hill, NJ is one of South Jersey's premier suburban markets — a densely populated township of ~81,000 residents with a median household income of $121,257 and 58% of adults holding bachelor's degrees or higher. The subject property at 1849 Route 70 E sits on Cherry Hill's highest-traffic commercial corridor, with Average Annual Daily Traffic (AADT) exceeding 48,000–84,000 vehicles depending on the segment. Route 70 is the township's primary retail spine, home to Heritage Square Shopping Center (directly adjacent at 1871 Route 70 E), the Tuscany Marketplace, Barclay Farm Shopping Center, and the massive Wonder Food Hall at Garden State Park.

The burger category in Cherry Hill is well-established with strong enterprise players — Shake Shack ($163,675 in DoorDash GOV over 3 months, $34.63 AOV), Five Guys ($90,848 GOV, $31.00 AOV), three McDonald's locations (combined $247,196 GOV), Red Robin ($42,600 GOV, $40.00 AOV), Habit Burger Grill ($33,166 GOV), and Wendy's ($41,791 GOV). However, there is a notable gap in the **smash burger** category specifically — Smashburger's Cherry Hill location has closed, and no dedicated smash burger concept currently operates in the market. The presence of high-performing burger chains validates strong consumer demand while the Smashburger closure creates a clear category opening.

**Bottom Line:** **RECOMMENDED** — Strong demographics, exceptional traffic counts, proven burger demand via DoorDash data, and a clear white space opportunity in the dedicated smash burger category make this an attractive location for BurgerRunn.

## Opportunities

1. **Smash Burger White Space** — Smashburger (closed), DaNick's (closed), and BurgerFi (closed) have all exited the Cherry Hill market. No dedicated smash burger concept remains, creating a clear category opening for BurgerRunn.

2. **Proven Premium Burger Demand** — Shake Shack's $163,675 in 3-month delivery GOV with a $34.63 AOV and 66% repeat rate proves Cherry Hill consumers actively seek and repeatedly purchase premium burgers. Five Guys adds another $90,848 at $31.00 AOV.

3. **Exceptional Traffic Counts** — Route 70 delivers 49,000–84,000 vehicles per day past the subject property, providing massive drive-by visibility and impulse-stop potential.

4. **Strong Student Population** — 16,140 K-12 students including 3,000+ high schoolers at two large high schools. Bishop Eustace Prep is directly on Route 70. After-school burger runs are a natural fit.

5. **Active Restaurant Market** — Wonder Food Hall (20 restaurants), Dave's Hot Chicken, Raising Cane's, and Paris Baguette all opened recently, demonstrating robust restaurant investment and foot traffic growth along Route 70.

6. **Affluent, Educated Consumer Base** — $121,257 median HH income and 58% bachelor's degree+ education rate indicate consumers willing to trade up for quality over fast food.

## Challenges

1. **Heavy Overall Burger Competition** — 10+ burger-serving restaurants within 3 miles including Shake Shack, Five Guys, Red Robin, Habit Burger, and three McDonald's. BurgerRunn must carve a distinct positioning.

2. **TD Bank Campus Transition** — The former TD Bank HQ campus at 1701 Route 70 E (adjacent to the subject property) has been sold. The captive corporate lunch audience at that exact location has diminished, though the campus buildings may be repurposed.

3. **Restaurant Closures Signal Challenges** — BurgerFi, DaNick's/FoodieHall, Iron Hill Brewery, and The Bistro have all closed recently in Cherry Hill. Concept execution and differentiation matter — the market rewards quality but is unforgiving to underperformers.

4. **Wonder Food Hall Competition** — The Wonder food hall at 2050 Route 70 (~0.2 mi away) includes "Burger Baby" and Bobby Flay Steak concepts, adding direct competition from a well-funded, multi-concept operator.

5. **Median Age Skews Older** — At 46.3 years, the median age is above the typical fast-casual sweet spot (25–44). However, the 16,140 students and family households provide balance.

## Success Factors

1. **Own the Smash Burger Category** — Position BurgerRunn as Cherry Hill's dedicated smash burger destination. Lean into what makes smash burgers different — crispy edges, Maillard crust, speed. The category name is literally open since Smashburger left.

2. **Price Between Five Guys and Shake Shack** — Target $10–$14 per burger to sit in the premium fast-casual tier. Five Guys averages $31 AOV and Shake Shack $34.63 — a slightly lower price point with comparable quality captures the value-conscious premium buyer.

3. **Maximize Delivery from Day One** — Cherry Hill's DoorDash market is massive (400,000+ orders, $13.5M+ GOV in 3 months). Launch with DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub on day one. Shake Shack generates $55,000/month in delivery alone.

4. **Capture the Student Rush** — With 3,000+ high school students, 800+ middle schoolers, and Bishop Eustace Prep on Route 70, an after-school (2:30–5pm) promotional window could drive significant incremental volume. Consider student-friendly combo pricing.

5. **Leverage Route 70 Visibility** — Invest in bold, visible signage. Route 70 delivers 49,000–84,000 vehicles/day. A strong marquee or LED sign can convert passing traffic into first-time visitors.

6. **Build Repeat Business Early** — Enterprise burger brands in Cherry Hill achieve 50–73% repeat rates on DoorDash. A loyalty program or app-based rewards system should launch with the restaurant to capture and retain customers from the start.

## Final Recommendation

**RECOMMENDED**

Cherry Hill, NJ presents one of the strongest location opportunities for a burger concept in the South Jersey market. The combination of exceptional demographics ($121,257 median HH income, 58% college-educated, 81,000 population), massive traffic counts (49,000–84,000 AADT on Route 70), and a proven, high-volume DoorDash delivery market creates a compelling foundation. The subject property at 1849 Route 70 E benefits from adjacency to Heritage Square Shopping Center and the growing Route 70 restaurant corridor.

Most critically, there is a clear **smash burger white space** opportunity. With Smashburger, DaNick's, and BurgerFi all having exited the market, no dedicated smash burger concept operates in Cherry Hill. Yet Shake Shack alone proves that premium burger consumers are here and spending — $163,675 in delivery GOV in just 3 months with a 66% repeat rate. The total burger delivery market exceeds $660,000/quarter across tracked stores, and that represents only the delivery channel.

The challenges are real — heavy overall burger competition, the Wonder Food Hall's Burger Baby concept nearby, and a market that has punished underperformers. BurgerRunn will need to execute at a high level on food quality, speed, delivery operations, and marketing. But for a brand that delivers a differentiated smash burger experience at a competitive price point, this location offers the traffic, demographics, and consumer demand to support strong performance.
