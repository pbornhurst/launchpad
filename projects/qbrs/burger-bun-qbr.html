Key Findings

  1. Exceptional top-line growth. Total sales grew +133.9% QoQ to $92,708.77 with orders up +120.7%. Every channel contributed — in-store +136.6%, Marketplace +87.1%, and Storefront +2,854% off a small base. AOV moved up from $25.76 to $27.29 (+5.9%).
  2. Menu conversion is on a remarkable upward trajectory. Online menu conversion rate climbed from 7.79% the week of Dec 29 to 22.89% by end of March — nearly 3x. Photo coverage also rose from 75% to 81%, and menu visitors grew +116% QoQ.
  3. Sponsored Listings is your most efficient growth lever. ROAS improved from 4.44x (Q4) to 5.35x (Q1), cost per acquired order dropped to $5.24, and the program drove 52 new customers. Impressions nearly doubled while CTR remained healthy at 5.13%.
  4. The Loyalty Promo is a retention engine, not an acquisition one. 138 orders and $4,652 in sales, with 126 of 127 identifiable customers being existing/repeat. At $1,296 in your-funded discount, it is doing exactly what a loyalty promo should — preserving repeat business.
  5. Storefront (1st party) is still small but accelerating. Growing from 5 orders in Q4 to 114 in Q1 with a $35.99 AOV — the highest across all channels. This is your highest-margin digital channel and represents untapped potential.
  6. In-store discount discipline is excellent. Only 1.29% discount rate in-store while growing 137% QoQ. Marketplace discounting became more efficient (9.68% → 4.21%) while orders still grew 86%.
  7. Menu data cleanliness issue. Several top-selling items appear under two category names. Consolidating into one canonical category set would clean up the customer-facing menu experience.
  8. Ratings data is not yet aggregating. Marketplace delivery volume has grown past 180 orders in Q1 but the data warehouse has not yet surfaced aggregate ratings — the quality signal we do have (0% missing/incorrect) is strong.

Recommendations

  1. Double down on Sponsored Listings. ROAS is up 20.5% QoQ. Increase the daily Sponsored Listings budget by 25–40% to capture more new-customer volume at or near current efficiency. Target: 75+ new customers in Q2 via SL (up from 52).
  2. Continue the menu photo coverage push. You added 6 percentage points of coverage in Q1 (75% → 81%) and conversion responded directly. Target 90%+ photo coverage across your top-50 items by end of Q2 — focus on The Burger Bloc and Stacked & Loaded Sandwiches items first.
  3. Consolidate duplicate menu categories. Unify the emoji-prefixed and plain-text versions of your main categories (The Burger Bloc, Signature Sides, Breakfast Bliss, From the Greek Islands) into a single category tree. This will clean up the customer browsing experience.
  4. Scale Storefront. Your 1p online ordering channel grew from 5 to 114 orders and has the highest AOV ($35.99) of any channel. Promote your Storefront URL on in-store receipts, to-go bags, and social media — this bypasses marketplace commission.
  5. Extend Mexican Daily Lunch Specials. This Q1 campaign drove 32 orders at $26+ AOV with 6 new customers. Consider expanding to a weekend or dinner version.
  6. Expand Kids Menu visibility. Kids Menu combined drove $1,466 in Q1 revenue with 110 units sold. Feature kids items in Sponsored Listings during dinner hours (5–8pm) to capture family-dining demand.
  7. Re-evaluate the breakfast lineup. The Monster Hash Breakfast Burrito is a standout at approximately $1,665 in Q1 revenue. Breakfast Bliss drove $2,619 total. If your kitchen can support it, extending breakfast reach (earlier start time, weekend brunch) could unlock another revenue stream.
  8. Set up in-store customer identification. Your in-store business is 76% of revenue but has no customer-level attribution today. Connecting in-store orders to a loyalty or SMS program would enable retargeting and full-journey measurement.

Growth Opportunities

  1. Marketplace has room to run. At 576 orders in Q1 with a $31.22 AOV, Marketplace is still 19% of sales. Given your strong menu conversion trajectory and improving Sponsored Listings ROAS, there is headroom to 2x this channel over the next two quarters.
  2. Breakfast expansion. Breakfast Bliss generated $2,619 in Q1. A targeted breakfast Sponsored Listing campaign (6am–11am) could capture the morning daypart that most restaurants underserve.
  3. Halal menu differentiation. Your "100% Pure Halal Options" and gyro offerings are a brand differentiator in your market. Featuring this prominently in marketing copy could attract a defined customer segment.
  4. Catering / Large-Order. Given your AOV and diverse menu spanning burgers, Mexican, and Greek, you have the ingredients for a large-order / catering play. 12-piece wings, taco trays, and sandwich platters would extend you into the lunch-meeting and party daypart.
  5. DashPass promotion conversion. Your DashPass pickup credit drove 39 orders from 13 new customers in Q1 at zero direct spend. Scaling similar DoorDash-funded promotions is a high-leverage way to grow without increasing your own discount spend.
