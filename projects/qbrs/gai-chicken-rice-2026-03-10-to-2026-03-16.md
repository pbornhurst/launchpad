## Key Issues & Concerns

1. **Significant Week-over-Week Volume Decline (-18.7% card orders, -14.3% card GOV):** Both locations experienced notable declines compared to the prior week. Market St dropped from 528 to 433 card orders (-18.0%), and Castro dropped from 322 to 258 (-19.9%). This is a concerning trend that warrants investigation -- possible causes include weather, local events, or seasonal patterns. The decline is broad-based across both locations, suggesting a macro factor rather than a store-specific issue.

2. **Marketplace Cancellation Rate (100%):** All 8 Marketplace portal orders across both locations show as cancelled. With $22,338.70 in Marketplace subtotal flowing through the financial transaction data and 605 marketing-attributed Marketplace orders, there is a clear disconnect. The portal `is_cancelled_int` flag may be capturing a different metric than expected, or the store may be systematically cancelling certain order types. This needs clarification with the data team.

3. **Marketplace Discount Rate at 11.1%:** The combined Marketplace discount rate of 11.1% (driven by $2,315 in mx-funded discounts on $22,339 subtotal) exceeds the 8% threshold. The "20 save 3" and "$25 get $5 off" self-serve promos are the primary drivers. While these are generating strong ROAS (7.4x-7.9x), the mx is funding significant discounts.

4. **Storefront Channel Essentially Dormant:** Only 1 Storefront order in the portal data and $1,369.25 in Storefront transaction subtotal (primarily Castro at $1,246.25). Given the strong brand and customer base, the 1st-party online ordering channel represents untapped potential.

5. **Declining Trailing 7-Day Metrics:** Market St's trailing 7-day card orders dropped from 528 (Mar 3) to 433 (Mar 16) -- a consistent downward trajectory throughout the week. Castro similarly declined from 322 to 258. This erosion needs monitoring to determine if it is a temporary dip or a trend.

## Growth Strategy

1. **Storefront / 1st-Party Channel Expansion:** With Castro generating $1,246.25 in Storefront transaction subtotal but only 1 portal order, there is clear demand for direct ordering. Market St has minimal Storefront presence ($123). Recommend activating Storefront marketing (social media, in-store signage with QR codes, email campaigns) to capture more 1st-party orders at higher margins (no Marketplace commission).

2. **Kiosk Channel Optimization:** Both locations have strong Kiosk revenue ($5,012.50 Market St, $4,941.50 Castro) with zero discount rate. This is pure-margin revenue. Explore expanding kiosk capacity or promoting kiosk ordering during peak hours to reduce counter congestion and increase throughput.

3. **Menu Consolidation (Market St):** Market St has 81 SKUs across 13 categories with multiple overlapping menu versions (Entrees, GAI Entrees, GAI Lunch Special, GAI Western Entrees). The same "Original Gai" item appears in 3 different categories at 3 different price points ($21.15, $16.46, $16.58). Recommend consolidating to reduce complexity, improve kiosk/online ordering UX, and simplify kitchen operations.

4. **New Customer Retention Program:** Marketing acquired 136 new customers this week across both locations. Implement a retention strategy (e.g., a "come back" promo targeted at week-old new customers) to convert one-time visitors into regulars. Currently, the repeat customer data is minimal in portal (only 2 repeat CX identified), suggesting most marketing-acquired customers may not be returning.

5. **Catering / Large Order Opportunity:** The "Combo Meal with Drink" item averages $26.29-$28.97 -- the highest AOV items in the mix. With strong chicken rice products and proven operational quality (0.16% item error rate), Gai is well-positioned for catering or large-order programs. Discuss DoorDash for Business / catering setup.
