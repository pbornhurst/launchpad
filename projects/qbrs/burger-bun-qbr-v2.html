Executive Summary

Q1 2026 was a strong growth quarter for Burger Bun. Total sales reached $92,659.83 across 3,418 completed orders — a +134.0% quarter-over-quarter jump from $39,605.74 in Q4 2025. Growth was concentrated in-store: POS/cashier sales rose from $29,856.26 to $69,627.05 (+133.2%), and order count grew from 1,229 to 2,674 (+117.6%). Sponsored Listings produced a 5.35× ROAS on $947.78 of ad spend, delivering $5,070.93 in attributed sales. Storefront (1P online ordering) scaled from effectively idle in Q4 ($138.88 / 5 orders) to $4,102.72 / 114 orders at the highest AOV of any channel ($35.99). Kiosk launched late in the quarter with 50 orders at a $18.97 AOV.

The operational standout: menu conversion climbed from 7.79% in week 1 to 22.89% in the final week — nearly a 3× improvement, driven by better menu structure and rising photo coverage (75% → 81%).

Key Findings

  1. Growth is broad and accelerating. Total sales more than doubled QoQ (+134.0%) with in-store volume rising from 1,229 to 2,674 orders (+117.6%). Storefront scaled nearly 30× from a near-idle state.
  2. Menu conversion tripled through the quarter. The online funnel climbed from 7.79% in early January to 22.89% by end of March — visitors more than doubled and each visitor is far more likely to convert.
  3. Sponsored Listings is your strongest paid channel. 5.35× ROAS on $947 of spend, up +0.91× QoQ; 52 new customers at CPA $18.23. Worth scaling.
  4. The Loyalty Promo is carrying promo volume — largely on existing customers. 138 of ~260 promo orders (53%) and 126 of those 138 from existing customers. Strong retention engine; light on net-new acquisition.
  5. Mexican cuisine classification is likely a mismatch. The store is named "Burger Bun" and 47% of top-15 revenue comes from burger-category items; Marketplace customers searching "burgers" may never see you.
  6. Duplicate menu categories are dragging menu clarity. Six category pairs appear twice (emoji vs. non-emoji), likely a legacy POS sync artifact.
  7. Kiosk launched but hasn't yet scaled. 50 orders in Q1 at an $18.97 AOV — driving kiosk awareness and a clearer upsell flow is the biggest lever there.
  8. Rating capture is zero. No rated orders all quarter — no customer-satisfaction signal to act on.

Recommendations

  1. Correct the Marketplace cuisine classification from "Mexican" to "Burger" (or a burger-forward dual tag). Work with your DoorDash contact to update the primary cuisine. Expected impact: meaningful lift in Marketplace impressions and new-customer orders.
  2. Scale Sponsored Listings spend by ~50%. At 5.35× ROAS and an improving trajectory, raising the weekly budget from ~$75 to ~$110–$125 should keep ROAS above 4× while unlocking incremental orders.
  3. Pause the Alani Energy Drink B1G1 (0.28× ROAS) and scale the Mexican Daily Lunch Specials (4.77× ROAS). Mirror the lunch daypart structure with a "Burger Daily Lunch Specials" 11am–2pm.
  4. Rebalance the Loyalty Promo. 126 of 138 orders came from existing customers. Layer a new-customer-skewed offer (e.g., first-order $5 off $20) to bring the new:existing mix closer to the Sponsored Listings ratio (~1:2 rather than ~1:12).
  5. Consolidate duplicate menu categories. Merge "The Burger Bloc" / "The Burger Bloc (emoji)" and the other 5 duplicate pairs. Re-run the duplicate check after consolidation.
  6. Turn on post-order rating requests. Target at least 75+ rated orders in Q2 to establish a baseline satisfaction score.
  7. Grow kiosk attach. Kiosk AOV ($18.97) is ~27% below the in-store average ($26.04) — add combo/side upsell prompts and signage to close the gap.
  8. Double down on Storefront (1P). With a $35.99 AOV (highest of any channel) and ~29× QoQ growth, table-tent / receipt / social QR-code promotion for the 1P link will compound returns and improve your blended margin.

Growth Opportunities

  • Cuisine correction + SL scale-up is the biggest Q2 lever. Marketplace is the largest growth vector, and a correct cuisine tag plus 50% more ad spend could plausibly add $3k–$5k per month.
  • Storefront acquisition push. Storefront AOV ($35.99) is 16% above Marketplace ($31.00) — moving orders from Marketplace to Storefront improves margin while retaining the customer. A small 1P-only incentive (e.g., "$3 off your next 1P order") can pull repeat diners off Marketplace.
  • Lunch daypart promotions. Mexican Daily Lunch Specials hit 4.77× ROAS — replicate for burgers 11am–2pm.
  • Catering / large-order bundle. At a $27 AOV, there's headroom for an $80–$120 family/office bundle built around the Premium Chef Burger, Philly Cheesesteak, and Combo items.
