Purpose

DoorDash is exploring a potential partnership with Tab, a restaurant-centric corporate card and spend-management platform - conceptually similar to Ramp or Brex but purpose-built for restaurants. Today I ran two soft-launch discovery conversations (one with BurgerRunn, one with Cracked Eggery) to gauge appetite, identify the features that resonate, and surface friction with their incumbent tools. Both conversations were explicitly framed as exploration, not sales - no commitments asked for or offered.

What I walked both mx through

  • Corporate cards (physical + virtual) with per-location / per-category spend limits
  • Cash-back rewards on restaurant-related purchases (food vendors, supplies)
  • Back-office auto-capture: receipts and transactions auto-categorized into mx-defined codes; predictive vendor-to-code mapping (QuickBooks analog)
  • AI CFO layer: cash-flow projection, vendor-pricing change flags, spend optimization
  • Multi-location spend segmentation - ability to silo spend per store even on a single card

BurgerRunn (Mo) - Response

Current state

  • No business credit card today - BurgerRunn has been entirely self-funded and organic; Mo has never opened a business card
  • Had heard the Tab name but was not familiar with the product; not familiar with Ramp or Brex
  • Bookkeeper / accountant handles expense tracking manually today

Reaction

"Definitely would be interesting as we start growing a little bit more - to start keeping track of not just our stores, but helping everybody else that's open... it seems like something that would be a good fit for sure."
  • Positive - sees fit as BR grows and franchisees onboard
  • Franchisee angle resonated strongly - Mo explicitly called out that newer franchisees "might need a little bit more help and assistance on keeping track of their business as a whole"
  • Liked per-location spend limits and the concept of allocating $X to location A / monthly recurring spend to each store
  • Liked the debit-style mechanics (vs. true credit); spend limits felt like a natural fit for their franchisee model
  • Liked the AI CFO layer concept and auto-receipt capture / back-office categorization

Signal strength

Medium-to-high warm. Not an immediate buyer - they're operating fine without a corporate card today - but a clear "yes" on future fit as they scale. Franchisee economics is the strongest wedge: spend controls per franchisee location, with DD + BR visibility into the franchisee's spending, is a compelling sell.

Cracked Eggery (AJ) - Response

Current state

  • Already uses Ramp - but does not actually use Ramp's features
  • AJ tracks expenses manually via spreadsheet to split per store
  • Accountant is "old-school" - prefers manual reconciliation methods
  • Has a single GM (Fan) managing both CE locations - expense attribution by store is a daily pain point

Reaction

"I think I would definitely [be] interested to learn more about it for sure. Especially if it saves me time from... if I can set up codes and from historical data what I was going to categorize them as anyway, that would be great."
  • Positive - specifically because it's restaurant-centric
  • AJ's Ramp frustration is a structural one: "You could be a construction company, a restaurant... there's not many restaurant-specific products." A purpose-built restaurant tool answers that directly.
  • Strongest pull: time savings from auto-categorization based on historical patterns
  • Multi-location angle landed immediately - AJ lit up when I mentioned creating two different spend silos on a single card for their shared GM managing both stores
  • No friction on AI CFO or cash-back - both received as nice-to-have but not the primary hook

Signal strength

High warm. AJ has an active, unsolved problem (Ramp adoption has stalled, manual spreadsheet workflow is slowing his month-end) and Tab's restaurant-specific positioning is the exact differentiator he's missing. This is a displacement opportunity, not a greenfield one.
